☵ Water quality

Water contamination remains one of the most pervasive environmental health threats, with common contaminants including heavy metals, disinfection byproducts, microbial pathogens, pesticides, and more.

Exposure to heavy metals is linked to neurodevelopmental impairments, kidney dysfunction, and increased carcinogenic risk (WHO, 2017; Lanphear et al., 2018). Microbial pathogens can cause acute gastrointestinal illnesses (CDC, 2024). Disinfection byproducts, such as trihalomethanes (THMs) arising from chemical treatment of water, have been associated with developmental issues (Grazuleviciene, 2011) and more.

While research on complex mixture effects and low-dose chronic exposures continues to evolve, the precautionary principle supports implementing robust filtration systems capable of addressing multiple contaminant classes simultaneously.
